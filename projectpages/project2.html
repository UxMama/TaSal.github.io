MARVEL PROTOTYPE

PROBLEM

Herbal teas have been a big trend for the last few years and we see new ones hitting the shelves all the time. My client needed to solidify and requested an e-commerce store design to shed light on the possibilities of promoting and growing his product and brand. Health Hooch is a homemade herbal tonic with major health benefits The goal was to provide a user-friendly, aesthetically appealing online store, and an easy shopping experience where consumers can shop for the product but also gain knowledge about the ingredients and benefits. The functional, clean design as well as strong brand development will be introduced to to investors, and manufactures. My client also plans to apply the design to his designated e-commerce platform.

SOLUTION

After going over details of the project and having in-depth conversation about the brand I was able to provide a user-friendly, bright and organized online store design. I also developed a brand that sends a message of strength and health. Health Hooch not only provides organic and safe products to consumers but they also learn while they're shopping. The store gives users an option to subscribe to a monthly plan or shop as guests giving them more purchasing power and freedom, which we believe will result in loyal subscribers.

UNDERSTANDING THE USERS

DISCOVERY &
RESEARCH

I created a user survey to learn more about who the users are, what they shop for online and their interest and knowledge of health products. I also learned about what their motivations are when it came to searching and using health products. Online shopping experience was also important, so I had my users share their success and failures of online shopping. These questions were shared through online forums, social media, and sent out via text.

PERSONA
CREATION

With the data I collected from surveys, I was able to create the perfect personas for Health Hooch. These personas are model users that the I used to understand the goals, motivations, and behaviors of the people who will use the interface.

COMPETATIVE
ANALYSIS

The health drink trend is a very competative market. Although after a ton of research, I found that several brands including my client's, can still stand strong next to other established brands and still stand out from eachother in many ways. With the help of the competative analyis, I found several undeserved markets that Health Hooch could take on.

USER FLOWS &
USER STORIES

User flows were created to analyze efficiency of tasks that would be performed on the store site. I also felt that it would help developers understand how the system interacts with user behavior and assisted me in designing a simpler navigation experience.

INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE

SKETCHING &
WIREFRAMES

In order to visualize the screen-to-screen interface for Health Hooch I started with pen and paper. I moved on to refining the concepts of how the e-commerce site would work through wireframing. This allowed me to go from thinking about the basic features to thinking in higher detail, such as where sizing, placement and order of elements would be.

USABILITY
TESTING

An e-commerce store can be a daunting task not only to create but to visit as a consumer. I performed multiple tests with users to gain a better understanding of their movements in an e-commerce store and to find out which areas needed more work. I started with the paper prototype for many reasons; it invloves the user at an early stage,rapid evaluation and testing and assistance in documentation. After paper prototypes I had users test with an invision prototype.

VISUAL DESIGN

BRANDING

Branding was very interesting! I had the chance to really dive into the history of Hooch. I could see where the product stood out compared to it's competitors. Hooch is originally a potent alcoholic beverage usually made in the woods because it is highly illegal. Names that most people are familiar with are: Moonshine, white lightning, mountain dew, choop, homebrew, shiney, or white whiskey. With that in mind, we knew that the route we wanted to take Health Hooch was a rugged, dark and natural theme.

LOGO
DESIGN

I performed market research on 4 other logo designs from our competitors. Once I figured out the direction we were aiming for, my client and I took another look at a few more logos from competitors and incorporated things that we liked from each. This was strictly experimental and helped us learn what we liked, what worked and what respresented the brand best. I moved on to sketches and worked our way down to 3 final sketches and placed them on packaging mockups to make our final decision. The current logo name is enclosed inside large typeface of the same letter, simple but creative, this worked well for marketing and trending names like "HH". A ring around the name was added, which represents the bottom of a mason jar, which hooch is closely associated with. To ensure our users understood that this is a healthy product I added custom vector leaves at the bottom of the ring.

COLOR &
TYPOGRAPHY

The color palette was fairly easy to accomplish, although there was hesitation on different shades of each color selected. We wanted to ensure that we were capturing the boldness of Health Hooch through it's colors. So nature colors were selected but darker hues were chosen to represent the rugged, and woodsy feeling.

A/B
TESTING

I went through several versions of Health Hooch, particularly the landing page which contained all of the information a consumer would need when visiting a new store. I took this approach to eliminate having to visit several different pages to find the products or educational resources they need. These two versions were tested as we were also trying to decided on possible packaging products. The 3 Bottle landing page blew version 1 out of the water! Users loved the sleek look and really enjoyed the liquor bottle packaging for a non-alcoholic health drink.
