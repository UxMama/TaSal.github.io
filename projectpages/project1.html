Project Summary

Cloud storage users want a safe and reliable space to store their memories, work, music and more. There are many options out there, but users still face a number of frustrations. My research showed that users want better security measures, and a reliable system that won't fail them if their wifi goes down. Complicated organization, and saving links are among those frustrations and easy collaboration was also crucial to work teams.

Solution

My research indicated a highly diverse audience that represented every kind of user. With extensive research the solution resulted in an app that inspired easy organization through folders and categories only. PUSH provides a secure offline version so users can work offline,and when it comes to our users feeling safe while sharing, they can depend on several layers of security. These features include expiration and a 6 digit custom code.

Competitive
Analysis

Where would PUSH enter and stand on the market against other powerhouse storage systems that already exist? In the competitive analysis, I compared the apps which participants currently use for cloud storage and organization. Check out the Competitive Analysis details here.

The competitive analysis assisted me in finding the opportunities to take when compared to other big name storage products. While creating PUSH, and focusing on functionality and the experience our users needed, I also decided that this product would offer:

• Compatible across all operating systems

• Provide faster speeds

• Digital codes for every shared file along with notifications of when your file is being viewed and by who.

Sketching &
Wireframes

I started with sketches and cycled through iterations after several testing sessions with paper prototypes. I moved onto creating wireframes in Figma and then put them to work by testing several times with participants. On the low fidelity wireframes, some participants found that navigating was easy and understood the product’s purpose. I moved on to creating hi-fi wireframes and the feedback indicated that the navigation system was not as clear, participants were confused about whether the left navigation were links or buttons. You can check out the wireframes here in Figma.

Usability
Testing

I performed 5 usability and preferences tests, resulting in feedback that would deeply impact the final product and also resulting in two rounds of iterations. Testing was done remotely and in-person. Using clickable prototypes and retesting several times would ensure the design was user-friendly.

After I conducted the usability tests for the prototype, there were still a few things that needed to be improved to ensure a seamless experience. Learn more about the users here.

ROUND 1: I cycled through navigation systems for the landing pages a few times. I finally found a system that was familiar, functional, using a top navigation through a breadcrumb style navigation. I also decided to use images that were much more relatable to individuals who would use this type of product. Push went from multi-colored gradients, abstract backgrounds, and illustrations, to powerful photography and strong content that connected the users to the product.



ROUND 2: I added color and typography and created clickable prototypes with invision and tested the first version of my design once again.The participants were excited by the colors at first glance but it soon proved to be overstimulating.
The gradients caused confusion and users were so distracted. The feedback also showed that the navigation was not a solid, users did not understand whether these were links, tabs, buttons or what would happen when they clicked. I decided to use only two colors for gradient, and subtle highlight indicator for the selection they were on in the dashboard. This tested very well and users were much more pleased with the Navigation and the less distracting but beautiful color scheme.

UI & Branding

Branding

The name PUSH was inspired by some of the participants. I asked three simple questions to provoke more freedom during their thought process. Instead of overwhelming them with a ton of questions, I wanted to focus on the end goal which is how they feel or how they connect to the product they are using. What do you think of when using a cloud storage? What do you think of when it comes to WHY you are using the cloud storage app? What emotion do you associate with it?

PUSH represents the motivation, confidence and connection that users want to achieve and experience while using this product. It also represents the literal pushing of files, documents, links, videos etc into the cloud. From there, things were moving on up and my product started to take shape,but not without trial and error.

Logo
Design

Emphasis was placed on the simple word “push” by using all capitals. From there I incorporated the upload symbol that the world is so familiar with, and combined it with the letter “H”. Users found it creative and quickly understood the meaning of the logo and symbol. The feedback on this logo was great, they applauded my efforts in keeping the brand identity strong throughout the entire design

The Sky Is Your Limit

Reflection

Through my design process I found that overstimulating users is possible when you try to fix something that is broken. In this case, it happened to be my use of navigation and gradients. These distractions and overstimulation resulted in a poor experience for my users. I became overly concerned with trends, and visuals that I did not have much experience using, only to crash and burn.

After revisiting my surveys, analysis and performing more testing I came back to what the goal actually was, and what solution was needed. I revamped the entire product from some of the major wireframes to the icons, buttons and color palette. I implemented familiarity when it came to using crumb navigation to keep it simple. I also used best practices for gradients to highlight important aspects of the product and to add depth to the dashboard.

The users instantly felt the growth of the project and dove right in without hesitation, it was as if they used the product before. It was clean, easy and aesthetically appealing. I constantly called, chatted and asked questions and requested explanations. My biggest accomplishment is when I realized how much value there is in allowing the users to co-create.
