PUSH

Summary

Push Cloud storage is a unique tool used by approximately over 2 million people all over the world,
and it that number continues to increase as the needs of users grow. People use cloud storage both
their personal and professional lives.The goal of PUSH is to provide a seamless user experience and
appealing design that fits the needs of all users no matter what their lifestyle is. My process for this
project entailed entensive research, persona creation, User stories and sitemaps. In Figma
I developed Low and Hi fidelity wireframes and mockups as well as an interactive prototype using Invision.

My Roles for this project included UX/UI Design and Research, Branding Visual Design,and Interaction Design.
Deliverables included Layered design file for all mockups, Static assets, Clickable prototype, Branding details,
SVG and native file format, layered style guide, SVG and PNG Black-and-white versions of all logo variations,
SVG and PNG Color versions of all logo variations, User testing, wireframes, and competative analysis.

Problem

When it comes to cloud storage users
want a safe and reliable space to
store their memories, work, music etc.
Though there are many options out there,
users still found faced a number of
frustrations.Through my research I found
that users are still seeking an app that
offers multiple layers of security ,and a
reliable system that won't fail them if their
wifi goes down. Organization sometimes
became headaches for users and a decrease
in productivty becomes an issue when they
don’t have access to collaboration methods.

Solution

My research indicated a highly diverse
audience that represented every kind of
user.I had to ensure the Cloud storage app
met their needs. With extensive research the
solution resulted in an app that inspired
easy organizationthrough Folders and Categories
only. PUSH provides a secure offline version
so users can work offline,and when it comes to
our users feeling safe while sharing, they
can depend on several layers of security.
These features include expiration and a
6 digit custom code.

Competitive Analysis

In order to dictate where PUSH would
enter and stand on the market against other powerhouse storage
systems that already exist, in-depth research was necessary.
For the competitive analysis, I used the apps which
participants in my survey, currently use for cloud storage
and organization.

We found their strengths to
include having nothing to download, easy sharing, auto
saving, and a free option. We found weaknesses to be
primarily a lack of ability to save web content. We
took these results into account when deciding on
our app structure.

User Stories & Flows

Based on the data collected, and utilizing the

user stories to highlight the main features of this
product. To obtain a clearer picture of organizing
the movements of users, I converted them into User

Usability Testing

I performed remote and in-person testing sveral times
as well with a clickable prototype to ensure the design
was user-friendly and so that I could iterate and
improve the design as needed. The tasks that were
performed were:

Task #1: Create an account
Task #2: Upload a document
Task #3: Add a file to a folder

Clickable

After I conducted the usability tests for my prototype,
I was very happy with the results. Although there were a
few things that needed to be improved to ensure a seamless experience.

• Create a smoother navigation system. The users navigated easily
although the location of the navigation paired with the encasing
on a rectangular shape looks as if they could be buttons or links.
• Shorten the length of the list when the + is activated to create, upload etc.
I noticed hesitation on the users end when the drop-down list was presented
and I want to eliminate that feeling.
• Add another + in the list of files section and designate that as a create
a new folder or category for organization.
• The sign-up page was confusing for the first few users. They were confused
about whether they should fill in the fields and then select the plans -OR-
review and select the plans and move onto completing the information fields.
• Create icons that are complimentary sizes for sizes for the label buttons,
previews of folders, files, and categories.

Branding & Identity

The name PUSH was inspired by some of my participants. I thought
it was suitable to keep it simple by asking them three questions.
I wanted to focus on the end goal which is how they feel or how
they connect to the product they are using. I only asked three
questions: What do you think of when using a cloud storage?
What do you think of when it comes to WHY you are using the cloud
storage app? What emotion do you associate with it.

PUSH represents the motivation, confidence and feelings that users want to
achieve and experience while using this product. It also represents the literal
pushing of files, documents, links, videos etc into the cloud. From there,
things were movin’ on up and my product started to take shape, but not without
trial and error.

Logo Design

Logo Design.I decided to place emphasis on the simple word “push” by using all capitals. From there I incorporated the upload symbol that the world is so familiar with, into the “H”. I thought it was pretty clever and users got it right away!

Color Palette & Typography

PUSH Evolved from blue, teal, pink and purple gradients to a clean medium blue, use of calming grays and a splash of accent colors like yellow. I wanted my users to feel calm, less stressed and Confident using this product. The use of white space really helped clean up the design and allowed for more functionality and better readability.

Since I went with the concept of “Pushing files” I wanted a really fun techie typeface, Share Tech fit the bill perfectly. I paired this with a simple, easy to read and beautiful typeface, Montserrat. Share Tech is used mostly for headlines and subheads and all of text following is Montserrat, the combination engages the users and allows them to find and understand the information efficiently.

Preference Testing

I performed several usabilities and preferences tests, resulting in feedback that would deeply impact the final product. We cycled through navigation systems, I kept missing something there and finally found a system that was familiar, functional and created an easy and pleasing experience. I removed things like rounded corners, shadows, and anything that dated the app. My love for gradient became a disaster but once removed we had a clean, relaxing feel to the design. The gradient really distracted users and made it hard and confusing to figure out what they needed to do next. I went from gradients, abstract background, to using illustrations and finally decided on using powerful photography to help my users feel connected to the product.

77% say: Better readability

22% say: Hard to read

14% say: This landing page was a bit too much with gradient and array of floating shapes

86% say: This landing page made them feel connected to the product and was easier to nvigate around.

Final Product

After several rounds of in depth testing and iterations, I finally reached the final product that is clean, easy and aesthetically appealing that meets every users’ needs.

What I learned

The cloud storage is a booming industry and it doesn’t look like it’s slowing down any time soon. Designing and creating outstanding products cannot be taken lightly nor can we settle for mediocre apps that do not make the lives of our users easier. The competition is tough, really tough. Although the benefit of competition is that we discover our strengths and weaknesses and realize that we are capable of creating a better product by being different and putting our users first, maintaining user-centered mindsets and implementing design thinking. Through my design process I found that overstimulating users can be a majro distraction, so much so that their experience with the product is poor. I quickly became obsessed with what’s trending, what looks cool, what are other designers doing, how can I make this thing as colorful as possible? To finally come to the end, crash and burn. After revisiting my surveyed, analysis and doing more testing I came back to what the goal was, what solution I needed. I revamped the entire product from some of the major wireframes (Landing page) to the icons, buttons and color palette. The users went insane, they instantly felt the growth of the project and dove right in without hesitation, it’s as if they used the product before, they loved it. It was clean, easy and aesthetically appealing. I constantly called, chatted and asked questions and requested explanations. I allowed them to co-create with me and that was my biggest accomplishment, by doing this I was able to jump into the minds of my users beyond testing and interviews and let them guide the creation of PUSH, I just did the dirty work, happily course. Now I look at the original vision and though it’s embarrassing that I even thought that it could possibly work! I literally placed my users in a psychedelic hologram but I’m proud to say I had major growth and was able to go back to square one and in life my users.
